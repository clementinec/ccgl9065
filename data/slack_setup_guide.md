## How It Works

### Before Class (Homework)

1. **Research** the week's topic
2. **Add findings** to your personal Notion page
3. **Post to Slack** â€” share your best fact/story to the battlefield channel
4. **Tag your position:** `[PRO CLIMATE]` or `[PRO DEVELOPMENT]`

### During Class

**First 50 minutes:** Instructor presents facts, stakes, and angles of attack for both sides.

**Second 50 minutes:**
1. Students find others with similar positions
2. Groups synthesize their best arguments
3. Groups present to the class
4. Debate between sides

### After Class

- Best contributions get added to the **Channel Canvas**
- Students can continue discussing in the channel
- Browse other weeks' channels for ideas

## Why This Structure?

| Benefit | How |
|---------|-----|
| **No lock-in** | Students choose their position each week |
| **Cross-pollination** | Everyone can browse all channels |
| **Accountability** | Contributions are visible and attributed |
| **Portfolio building** | Notion page grows all semester |
| **Natural clustering** | Passionate people self-select |
| **Living archive** | Canvas preserves the best arguments |

---

## Instructor Workflow

### Before Each Week
1. Create the battlefield channel (`#w2-battlefield-food`)
2. Post the topic, key questions, and due date for pre-class posts
3. Pin the Slack post template

### During Class
1. Present facts and angles (first 50 min)
2. Facilitate group formation and discussion (second 50 min)
3. Moderate presentations and debate

### After Class
1. Curate best contributions into the Channel Canvas
2. Highlight great examples of fact-checked spectacles
3. Call out any "fake news" attempts (teachable moments)
